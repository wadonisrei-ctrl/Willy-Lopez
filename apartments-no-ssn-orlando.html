The Problem: Most Apartments Require an SSN You May Not Have Yet

If you have recently arrived in the US on a work visa, a student visa, or are in the process of applying for a Social Security number, you have probably run into the same wall over and over: most apartment applications require an SSN before they will even run your application.

This is not a legal requirement, it is a policy choice most leasing offices make because their standard credit-check software is built around SSN lookups. It shuts out a lot of qualified renters who simply have not received their SSN yet or are not eligible for one under their visa category.

I work directly with a small number of Orlando communities that have an alternative process for exactly this situation, so you are not stuck waiting on paperwork before you can secure a place to live.

What's Available: M North Apartments

One of the communities I place renters in directly is M North Apartments at 3500 Millenia Blvd in Orlando, in the Millenia corridor near I-4. M North does not require an SSN to apply. A passport and visa documentation are accepted in place of one.

1-bedrooms start at $1,567 per month, 2-bedrooms at $1,749, and 3-bedrooms at $2,094. Security deposits are relatively low for qualifying applicants, typically ranging from $175 to $1,025 depending on your application results. There is currently a move-in special of one month free on 1-bedroom units.

The Millenia location puts you within a mile of the Mall at Millenia, IKEA, Target, and a large Publix, and about 10 minutes from downtown Orlando on I-4. It is a genuinely central, convenient location, not a compromise you make just to solve the documentation issue.

What You'll Need Instead of an SSN

Every leasing office runs this a little differently, but in general, expect to provide a valid passport, your current visa or immigration documentation, and proof of income. If you are newly employed, an offer letter or employment verification letter from your employer usually works in place of pay stubs if you have not been paid yet.

Some applicants without established US credit history are asked for a larger deposit in place of a credit check, or may need a guarantor. I will tell you exactly what a specific community requires before you apply, so there are no surprises partway through the process.

If you have an ITIN instead of an SSN, that can sometimes be used as well. I can confirm this with the leasing office directly rather than you having to find out after submitting an application fee.

Who This Is For

International students on F-1 visas who have not yet worked in the US and do not have an SSN.

Work visa holders (H-1B, L-1, O-1, and similar) who are newly arrived and still waiting on their SSN paperwork to process, which can take several weeks after arrival.

Recent immigrants who are eligible for an SSN but have not received it yet, and need housing in the meantime.

Anyone who has been turned away or discouraged from applying elsewhere because a leasing office would not consider their application without one.

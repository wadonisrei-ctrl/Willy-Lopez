What Makes Dr. Phillips Worth the Rent

Dr. Phillips is one of Orlando's most established neighborhoods, sitting in the southwest part of the city near Sand Lake Road, Turkey Lake Park, and the Restaurant Row dining strip. It has a reputation for good roads, low crime, and a high concentration of restaurants and retail within a short drive.

For renters, Dr. Phillips offers access to the major employment corridors without the urban density of downtown. The I-4 and SR-528 interchanges give you fast routes to Universal Studios, the airport, Disney World, and downtown Orlando from a genuinely suburban setting.

Universal employees, healthcare professionals, hospitality workers along I-Drive, and finance professionals with offices in the south Orlando area make up a large part of the renter base here.

The Dr. Phillips Rental Market

Dr. Phillips is one of the higher-rent areas in the Orlando metro. Apartments here tend to feature newer finishes, and the surrounding neighborhood and proximity to Restaurant Row push rents above what you see in Kissimmee, Davenport, or standard east Orlando.

What you get in return is a location that is genuinely convenient. SR-528 takes you to Orlando International Airport in under 20 minutes. Sand Lake Road puts you on one of the best restaurant strips in the city. I-4 connects you to Universal, downtown, and Disney.

I work with a community in the Sand Lake corridor near Dr. Phillips, close to SunRail: one-bedrooms from $1,700, two-bedrooms from $1,800, three-bedrooms from $2,200. If your budget is below that or you specifically need somewhere else in the immediate Dr. Phillips core, I do not currently have another match in this exact area, and I will tell you that honestly.

Good Fit for Universal Employees and I-Drive Workers

If you work at Universal Studios, Loews Hotels, or anywhere along International Drive, Dr. Phillips is one of the closest residential neighborhoods that still feels like a genuine neighborhood rather than a hotel corridor.

The drive from Dr. Phillips to Universal's front gate is typically under 10 minutes. International Drive is a similar distance in the opposite direction. For people who need frequent airport access, SR-528 makes MCO one of the most accessible airports-to-home trips in the metro.

I also cover the Millenia area just north of Dr. Phillips, with two communities there. I do not currently have coverage in the Windermere corridor itself. If you want to compare Millenia and Sand Lake side by side, tell me your work location and I will put together an honest comparison.

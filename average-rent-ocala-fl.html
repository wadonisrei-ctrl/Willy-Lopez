What Does It Cost to Rent in Ocala, FL?

Ocala is one of the more affordable rental markets in Florida. Compared to Orlando, Tampa, or Miami, renters get more space for less money. That is one of the main reasons people relocate here.

As of 2026, here are the general rent ranges across Marion County:

Studios: $800 to $1,050 per month One-bedroom apartments: $950 to $1,350 per month Two-bedroom apartments: $1,150 to $1,700 per month Three-bedroom apartments: $1,500 to $2,200 per month

These are ranges, not averages. A newer build in SE Ocala with a gym and pool will cost more than an older community in Silver Springs Shores. Location, amenities, and management company all affect price.

Rent by Neighborhood in Ocala

Where you live in Ocala matters a lot for price.

Silver Springs Shores is the most affordable part of Ocala. One-bedrooms run $900 to $1,100. Good for budget-conscious renters and families. The area is mostly suburban residential.

SE Ocala along SW 17th Street and the hospital corridor has newer construction and slightly higher rents. One-bedrooms average $1,100 to $1,400. Popular with healthcare workers at Advent Health and HCA Florida Ocala.

Downtown Ocala has seen new development. Rents for newer units run $1,200 to $1,600 for a one-bedroom. The tradeoff is walkability and proximity to dining and events.

Paddock Park and NW Ocala vary widely. Some communities are value-oriented, others are on the higher end. Count on $1,000 to $1,800 depending on the property.

What Is Included in Rent?

Most apartment communities in Ocala include water and trash in the monthly rent. Some include cable or internet packages. Electricity and renter's insurance are almost always separate.

Many newer communities charge separately for amenities like valet trash, pest control, and parking. These add-ons can add $50 to $150 to your monthly cost. Always ask what is included before signing.

Pet fees are also separate. Expect a one-time pet fee of $200 to $500 per pet, plus a monthly pet rent of $25 to $75.

Ocala vs. Orlando Rents

One of the reasons people leave Orlando for Ocala is cost. In Orlando, a one-bedroom apartment in a mid-tier community averages $1,400 to $1,800. In Ocala, the same bedroom type runs $950 to $1,350.

That is a savings of $400 to $600 per month, or $4,800 to $7,200 per year.

Ocala is about 80 miles north of Orlando. The commute is too far for most jobs, but for remote workers or those with jobs in Ocala, the savings are real.

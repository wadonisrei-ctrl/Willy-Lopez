The Ocala Seller Market Right Now

Ocala has been a seller-favorable market for several years running. Marion County is one of the fastest-growing counties in Florida, and that growth has driven consistent demand for homes across every price tier.

Median home prices in Ocala rose significantly from 2020 to 2023 and have stabilized at levels that gave many homeowners substantial equity they did not have five years ago. If you bought in Silver Springs Shores in 2019 for $190,000 and it is now worth $265,000, that is real money.

Well-priced homes in Ocala still move relatively quickly. Overpriced homes sit. The key is getting the pricing right at the start, which means looking at actual recent comparable sales, not automated estimates that may not account for your home's specific condition and location within the neighborhood.

What the Selling Process Looks Like in Florida

Selling a home in Florida follows a standard sequence, but there are Florida-specific things worth knowing before you list.

Florida requires sellers to disclose known material defects. This is not optional. If you know about a roof issue, a plumbing problem, a history of flooding, or any other material defect, it goes on the disclosure. This protects you legally and builds trust with buyers.

The documentary stamp tax on the deed is a seller cost in Florida. On a $270,000 sale, that is about $1,890. This is separate from the real estate commission and shows up in your closing costs.

Title insurance in Florida is typically split: the seller buys the owner's title policy, the buyer buys the lender's policy. On a $270,000 home, the owner's policy runs roughly $1,500 to $2,000.

The timeline from accepted offer to closing typically runs 30 to 45 days for a financed buyer, or 10 to 21 days for a cash buyer. Cash offers are common in the Ocala market, especially for entry-level and retiree-demographic homes.

How I Price Your Home

Pricing is the single most important decision in selling a home. Price it right and you get multiple showings and a fast offer. Price it too high and the home sits, accumulates days on market, and eventually sells for less than it would have with the right price from day one.

I price homes by pulling recent comparable sales within your neighborhood, adjusting for square footage, condition, upgrades, and lot characteristics. I do not use Zillow estimates as a baseline. I look at what buyers actually paid for homes like yours in the last 90 to 120 days.

I tell you the number honestly, even if it is lower than you were hoping for. An accurate price gets your home sold. An inflated price gets you weeks of showings that go nowhere.

Ocala Neighborhoods I Know Well

Each Ocala neighborhood sells differently, and knowing the differences matters when pricing and marketing your home.

Silver Springs Shores has high turnover and consistent buyer demand. First-time buyers, investors, and people moving from out of state all target this area. Days on market for well-priced homes here tends to be shorter than other Ocala neighborhoods.

Marion Oaks in SW Ocala attracts a similar buyer profile. Entry-level and investor demand is strong. Cash buyers are more common here than in higher price tiers.

SE Ocala along the 17th Street corridor draws healthcare workers and younger professionals. Newer construction creates some competition, but resale homes with updates move well.

Paddock Park and NW Ocala have a longer days-on-market compared to entry-level areas, but homes here sell at higher prices and attract a more established buyer. Marketing to out-of-state buyers is often effective in this tier.

Downtown Ocala condos and townhomes have a narrow but active buyer pool. Walkability and urban amenities are the pitch here.

Why Some Renters Want a House, Not an Apartment

A lot of the people I work with in St. Cloud are not looking for a unit in a building. They want a real house: their own yard, no shared walls, more square footage, and the feel of a neighborhood rather than a community with a leasing office.

This usually comes up with families who are growing out of an apartment, people relocating from out of state who want a trial run in a house before they commit to buying, or renters who simply prefer the privacy and space of single-family living.

St. Cloud, and specifically the Harmony West area, has a growing supply of newer single-family rental homes built for exactly this kind of renter.

How St. Cloud Rental Homes Compare to Apartments

The math is different for a rental home versus an apartment, and it is worth understanding before you decide which fits your situation.

Rental homes cost more per month than a comparable apartment, but you get significantly more space, your own yard, no shared walls, and usually a garage or dedicated parking. For a family of four or five, a 4-bedroom house at $2,722 can work out to a similar or better price per square foot than a 2-bedroom apartment at $1,700 to $1,800.

Application and approval work the same way as an apartment: income verification, credit check, and a standard lease. The main difference is what you are actually renting, not how you get into it.

If you are not sure whether a house or an apartment fits your budget and needs better, tell me your situation and I will walk you through both options honestly, including what is currently available in St. Cloud apartment communities if that ends up being the better fit.

Why Retirees Move to the Orlando Area

Florida is the top destination for retirees in the country, and Central Florida specifically has a few advantages over the more saturated South Florida market.

No state income tax. Florida has no personal income tax, which makes retirement income go further.

Lower cost of living than South Florida. Rents in the Orlando area run well below Miami or Fort Lauderdale for comparable units.

Warm weather year-round. The Central Florida climate is milder than the coast, without the constant humidity and hurricane risk of Southeast Florida.

Proximity to family. Many retirees follow adult children who moved to the Orlando metro for work in healthcare, tech, or the hospitality industry.

Season flexibility. You can choose to be here year-round or use a rental as a seasonal base and return north in summer.

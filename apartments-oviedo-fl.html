Why Renters Choose Oviedo

Oviedo is a Seminole County city about 20 miles northeast of downtown Orlando. It has some of the highest-rated public schools in Central Florida and a small-town feel that most Orlando suburbs do not offer. The revitalized downtown area at Oviedo on the Park has restaurants, a town green, and community events that make it genuinely walkable by Florida standards.

For renters, Oviedo offers a quieter lifestyle with fast access to UCF, the Research Park corridor, and SR-417. If you work at Siemens, Lockheed Martin, RBC Bearings, or AdventHealth East Orlando, living in Oviedo can cut your commute to under 20 minutes.

Rents are generally competitive with the east Orlando market. Not as low as Kissimmee, but not as high as Lake Nona or downtown Orlando.

What the Oviedo Apartment Market Looks Like

Oviedo has a range of apartment options: older garden-style communities closer to SR-434 and downtown Oviedo, and newer builds along the Alafaya and Tuskawilla corridors closer to UCF. The closer you get to the downtown area, the more stable the pricing and the more established the neighborhoods.

Most communities in this area are pet-friendly and offer 1, 2, and 3-bedroom floor plans. Amenities vary by property age, but several newer communities match what you would find in Lake Nona or the Millenia area at more competitive pricing.

I can check current availability and tell you which communities in the Oviedo area have move-in specials or shorter waitlists.

Good Fit for UCF and the Tech Corridor

Oviedo is a natural choice if you work at UCF, the University of Central Florida Research Park, or one of the tech and defense companies along the SR-417 corridor. The drive from Oviedo to UCF is typically 10 to 15 minutes, mostly on local roads.

For Research Park employees, Oviedo sits almost directly adjacent to the park and SR-417, which is one of the cleaner commutes in the metro. You avoid I-4 entirely depending on your specific work address.

If you want to compare options, I also cover Alafaya and parts of east Orange County near UCF. Give me your work address and I will factor commute time into the shortlist.

Moving to Oviedo from Out of State

Oviedo consistently shows up on relocation guides for families moving from the Northeast and Midwest. The school ratings, the small-town character, and the proximity to Orlando without the tourist congestion make it a careful, deliberate choice for out-of-state movers who have done their research.

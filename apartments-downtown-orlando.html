What Living in Downtown Orlando Actually Looks Like

Downtown Orlando is built around Lake Eola, Orange Avenue, and the SunRail commuter line. It is the most walkable part of the city by a wide margin, with restaurants, grocery stores, bars, and parks within walking distance for most buildings in the area.

For renters who want a city feel without paying New York or Miami prices, downtown Orlando delivers real urban living at a fraction of the cost. If your employer is in the Central Business District, you can walk to work. SunRail gives you a transit option for reaching Winter Park, Sanford, or the south corridor without touching I-4.

The renter profile here tends to be young professionals, remote workers, and people who chose Orlando specifically for the lifestyle and the low-tax environment.

The Downtown Orlando Apartment Market

Downtown Orlando has a mix of older high-rise buildings from the 1990s and 2000s and newer luxury mid-rises built in the 2010s and early 2020s. Thornton Park and Lake Eola Heights have some of the most desirable buildings, with lake views and walkable streets lined with independent restaurants and coffee shops.

Rents in the urban core are higher than most of the suburbs. In return, you get no car required for daily errands, more architectural character, and proximity to the entertainment and dining scene on Orange Avenue, Wall Street Plaza, and the Creative Village tech district.

Who Downtown Orlando Is Right For

Downtown Orlando works well for renters who prioritize walkability, a social scene, and urban convenience over maximum space. One-bedroom units tend to be smaller than what you get in the suburbs for the same price, but the tradeoff is location and quality of life.

If you work in a downtown law firm, government office, hospital, or a tech company in Creative Village, living in the core eliminates the commute entirely. You are also well-positioned for SunRail if your office is on the north or south corridor.

It is also a strong choice for people who are new to Orlando and want to get connected quickly. The density of activity downtown makes it easier to learn the city and find a social circle before you decide where to settle long-term.

Why People Choose Kissimmee for Their Apartment

Kissimmee sits in Osceola County just south of Orlando. It is the most affordable of the major Orlando-area rental markets, which makes it popular with first-time Florida renters, theme park employees, and families who want space without paying downtown Orlando prices.

The I-4 and US-192 corridors give you easy access to Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld, and the rest of the attraction belt. If you work in the parks, living in Kissimmee cuts your commute to under 20 minutes in most cases.

Rents are lower here than in Lake Nona, Winter Park, or downtown Orlando, and the communities tend to be newer than what you find in the older parts of the metro.

Kissimmee Is a Good Fit If You Work at Disney or the Theme Parks

Kissimmee is one of the closest residential areas to Walt Disney World. If you are a cast member, a CP participant, or you work in hospitality along US-192, living in Kissimmee cuts your commute down significantly.

The shift schedules in the parks mean you are often commuting outside of peak traffic hours, which makes the Kissimmee-to-Disney run even easier than it looks on a map.

Davenport, just west of Kissimmee, is another option for Disney and park workers. I have communities there as well, starting at $1,410 per month for a one-bedroom.

What I Do for Buyers

I am a licensed real estate agent based in Central Florida. I work with buyers in the Greater Orlando area and Ocala, helping them find the right home for their budget, timeline, and situation.

For buyers who are already renting in Central Florida, I often know them from their rental search. A lot of my rental clients come back when they are ready to buy. For buyers coming from out of state, I provide the local market knowledge that makes a long-distance purchase manageable.

My job is to make sure you understand what you are getting into before you sign anything. That means explaining how the process works in Florida specifically, how I get paid, what it actually costs to own in different areas, and which neighborhoods match what you are looking for.

Orlando vs Ocala: Which Market Is Right for You?

Orlando and Ocala are very different markets, and which one makes sense depends on your situation.

Orlando (Greater Metro) offers access to a large job market, theme parks, an international airport, and diverse neighborhoods across multiple counties. Per the Orlando Regional Realtor Association, the metro median sale price has been running roughly $370,000 to $400,000. Competition for well-priced homes is real, especially in Lake Nona, Winter Park, and Dr. Phillips.

Ocala is 90 minutes north and is one of the more affordable homebuying markets remaining in Florida. Recent market reports put the Marion County median in the $270,000 to $305,000 range. For remote workers, retirees, healthcare workers at the local hospitals, and buyers who want more home for the money, Ocala makes a strong financial case. I can pull current listings in your target range so you are working with today's numbers, not a general estimate.

If you are not sure which market fits your life better, tell me your situation and I will give you an honest read on both.

How Agent Compensation Works

Since the NAR settlement in August 2024, how buyer agents get paid has changed. Before I can show you homes, you sign a buyer representation agreement that specifies my compensation.

In most Central Florida transactions, the seller offers to cover the buyer's agent commission. I tell you upfront whether that is the case for any home you are interested in. If the seller does not offer compensation, we talk through your options before moving forward.

I do not hide this or bury it in the fine print. You will know exactly how I get paid before we start working together.

Out-of-State Buyers

A significant share of Central Florida buyers are relocating from out of state. New York, New Jersey, Chicago, Miami, and California are the most common origins.

For out-of-state buyers, a local agent is not optional, it is essential. You cannot spend a week driving neighborhoods. You need someone who can tell you which areas have good schools if that matters, which neighborhoods to avoid, which homes are overpriced relative to comparables, and how to make a competitive offer without being in the room.

I help out-of-state buyers navigate the process remotely. Video tours, FaceTime walkthroughs, and honest communication about what I am seeing are how this works. Most of my out-of-state clients close on homes they never visited in person until after their offer was accepted.

Florida-Specific Things Buyers Need to Know

Florida has a few things that are different from most other states.

The homestead exemption takes $50,000 off your home's assessed value for property tax purposes once it becomes your primary residence. Apply with the county property appraiser after closing.

Homeowners insurance is expensive in Florida and has gotten more expensive since 2022. Costs vary a lot by home age, roof condition, and flood zone, so get a quote before making an offer on any home rather than relying on a general estimate.

HOA fees are common. Many communities have them, some low, some significant. Always review the HOA documents before going under contract.

Florida closing costs for buyers typically include documentary stamp taxes on the mortgage note, title insurance, and lender fees. Budget 2 to 3% of the purchase price in closing costs beyond your down payment.

Why Cast Members Look at Davenport and Kissimmee

If you are starting a role at Disney, Universal, or one of the resort hotels, you do not need to live inside Orlando proper. Most cast members end up in Davenport or Kissimmee, both south and southwest of the parks, because rents are lower than closer-in Orlando neighborhoods and the commute is still manageable.

Davenport sits southwest of the Disney property and is one of the more affordable areas in the whole metro for newer construction. Kissimmee runs along US-192, the corridor most directly connected to the attractions area, and has a long history as a hospitality-worker neighborhood.

Both areas see a steady flow of new cast members, seasonal hires, and College Program participants moving in and out, so leasing offices in these corridors are used to shorter-notice move-ins and out-of-state applicants.

What's Available: Davenport

One community I work with in Davenport has 1-bedrooms starting at $1,419, 2-bedrooms at $1,795, and 3-bedrooms at $2,140 per month. Units have an in-unit washer and dryer and keyless entry. The standard lease term is 7 months minimum, which fits well for seasonal or program-length cast members who do not want a full year commitment. Pets are welcome.

Another community, also in Davenport, has 1-bedrooms from $1,410, 2-bedrooms from $1,894, and 3-bedrooms from $2,157. There is currently a move-in special of up to 2 months free. Pets are allowed, up to 2 with a combined weight limit of 100 pounds.

Both communities are a workable commute to the Disney property and give you more space for your money than anything closer to the parks.

What's Available: Kissimmee

The community I work with in Kissimmee has 1-bedrooms from $1,750, 2-bedrooms from $2,127, and 3-bedrooms from $2,719. There is currently a move-in special of up to 2 months free. Pets are welcome, up to 2 with a combined 100-pound limit.

Kissimmee puts you closer to the US-192 attractions corridor than Davenport, which matters if you are working at Universal or want a shorter drive on shift-change days.

I can tell you current availability and whether specials are still active, and exactly which community fits, when you reach out.

Why Lake Nona Is One of Orlando's Most In-Demand Rental Markets

Lake Nona went from orange groves to one of the nation's top-selling master-planned communities in about 15 years, repeatedly ranked among the top MPCs nationally by RCLCO and John Burns Real Estate Consulting, and Florida's top-selling MPC by that measure. It is now home to Orlando's Medical City, a cluster of hospitals and research institutions that includes Nemours Children's Health, the UCF College of Medicine, and the VA Medical Center.

That growth drives strong rental demand, especially from healthcare workers, researchers, and professionals who want to live close to where they work without the chaos of downtown Orlando.

The area is also popular with families because of the schools, the SunTrail trails network, and the overall planned-community feel. If you are relocating from a dense Northeast or Midwest city, Lake Nona often feels like a significant upgrade in space and quality of life.

Who Moves to Lake Nona?

Most of my Lake Nona leads fall into one of three groups.

Healthcare workers are the largest segment. If you are taking a position at Nemours, the VA, UCF Health, or any of the clinics in the Medical City campus, Lake Nona puts you within 10 minutes of work. It is the obvious place to look first.

Families relocating from out of state come here for the schools and the community feel. Lake Nona's planned design means everything is within a few miles, which matters if you are new to a city and do not want to spend your first year figuring out where things are.

Remote workers and tech professionals are a newer but growing segment. Lake Nona has attracted a handful of companies and has reliable infrastructure that draws people who want a quiet, green suburb but still want to feel like they are in a real place.

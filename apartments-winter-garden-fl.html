Why Renters Are Moving to Winter Garden

Winter Garden has grown quickly as a rental market in the Orlando metro. The Horizons West and Hamlin areas along the SR-429 corridor now have thousands of newer apartment units, most built in the last eight years. Streets are wider, schools are newer, and access to the theme parks is closer than most people expect.

For renters, Winter Garden checks a lot of boxes: newer construction, quieter residential character, and quick highway access via SR-429, I-4, and the Florida Turnpike. You can reach Disney World or Universal in under 20 minutes from most buildings in the corridor.

It is also one of the better options if you work in Windermere, Dr. Phillips, or southwest Orlando and want to cut your commute without paying premium rent in those zip codes.

What Apartments Look Like in Winter Garden

The Horizons West and Hamlin corridor has a large selection of newer apartment communities, most built between 2017 and 2024. You will find a mix of mid-rise and garden-style buildings, typically with resort amenities like pools, fitness centers, dog parks, and coworking spaces.

I work directly with a community in the Horizons West area, with one-bedrooms starting at $1,453, two-bedrooms from $1,819, and three-bedrooms from $2,189 per month. It is pet-friendly with no elevator, an 18-month standard lease, and move-in specials up to 2 months free depending on the floor plan.

If your budget is below roughly $1,453, or you want to compare more than one Winter Garden community side by side, I likely cannot cover that in this specific area right now, though other apartment listing sites will show more of the broader market. If you are comparing Winter Garden to Kissimmee or Davenport, expect to pay a bit more here. What you get in return is newer construction, better roads, and stronger school ratings in Orange County.

Great Location for Disney and Theme Park Employees

Winter Garden is one of the best options for Walt Disney World cast members and employees who want a residential neighborhood without living on the tourist corridor. You get distance from US-192 and the congestion around it while staying within easy commuting range of property.

SR-429 connects directly to the US-192 and SR-535 interchange near Disney, so the drive is mostly highway with few traffic lights through the residential stretch. Most cast members living here report commutes under 20 minutes during standard shift hours.

I also have coverage in Davenport and Kissimmee if you want to compare options that are closer to the parks or more affordable. Just let me know your work location and I will factor commute time into your shortlist.

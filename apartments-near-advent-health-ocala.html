The Hospital Corridor on SW 1st Avenue

Ocala has two major hospitals, and they sit less than a block apart on the same street. Advent Health Ocala is at 1500 SW 1st Ave. HCA Florida Ocala Hospital is at 1431 SW 1st Ave. If you are taking a position at either facility, or at any of the medical offices and specialty clinics that cluster around them, you are looking at the same rental market.

This concentration of healthcare employment on a single corridor makes the apartment search in Ocala very specific. You want to be close enough to keep the commute manageable on a 12-hour shift schedule, but not so close that you are paying a downtown premium for a location that is technically a hospital district.

The good news is that Ocala is a smaller city than Orlando or Tampa, which means commute times are shorter across the board. A 15-minute drive in Ocala is a reasonable worst case from most neighborhoods. The communities I work with put you within 10 to 20 minutes of the SW 1st Ave hospital corridor without putting you in the most expensive zip codes.

Which Ocala Neighborhoods Work Best for Healthcare Workers

NW Ocala is the closest residential area to the SW 1st Ave corridor. The drive is typically 8 to 12 minutes depending on traffic on College Road or 27th Avenue. Rents here are below the Ocala average, which makes it attractive for nurses and allied health professionals who are managing student loan payments alongside housing costs. Aurora St. Leon is one of the communities I place clients in here, with one-bedroom units starting at $1,349 per month.

SW Ocala along the Pine Avenue corridor is the other strong option. It puts you south of the hospital campus, which is a slightly longer drive (10 to 15 minutes) but keeps you in a newer construction belt with more amenity-rich communities. Pointe Grand Ocala is in this area, offering two-bedroom units starting at $1,375 per month. If you are a traveling nurse coming with a partner or roommate, that price point for a two-bedroom is well below what you would pay in Orlando or Tampa for the same setup.

SE Ocala along the 17th Street corridor is popular with healthcare workers who want a bit more of an established neighborhood feel. Slightly longer commute to Advent Health (15 to 20 minutes) but good road access and newer builds in the $1,100 to $1,500 range.

Travel Nurses and Out-of-State Relocators

A significant share of Ocala's healthcare workforce comes from out of state. Both Advent Health and HCA Florida Ocala pull from national nursing pipelines, which means a lot of the people searching for apartments near these hospitals are doing it from New York, Ohio, Texas, or California before their contract starts.

I work with this population regularly. The communities I represent in Ocala allow remote applications, which means you can submit your application, get approved, and have a lease signed before your start date without making a trip to Ocala first. I walk you through the process by phone or text, send floor plans and pricing, and let you know what current move-in specials are available.

For travel nurses specifically, lease flexibility matters. Standard leases in most Ocala communities run 12 months. If your contract is shorter, that creates a mismatch. I am upfront about this when you contact me rather than steering you toward a lease you cannot fulfill. For longer assignments or permanent placements, 12-month terms with the right community are often the better financial move versus furnished corporate housing.

What Apartments Near Advent Health Ocala Actually Cost

Ocala rents are significantly lower than most Florida markets. For a healthcare worker relocating from Miami, Tampa, Orlando, or out of state, the price comparison is usually a genuine surprise.

One-bedroom apartments near the Advent Health corridor run $1,100 to $1,600 per month for newer construction. Older communities are lower but tend to have more maintenance variability. The sweet spot for someone who wants a newer building, in-unit washer and dryer, and covered parking is roughly $1,350 to $1,500 per month.

Two-bedroom units run $1,375 to $1,800 for comparable quality. If you are splitting rent with a roommate or relocating with a partner, the per-person cost drops well below $1,000 a month in many cases.

Move-in costs are typically first month plus a deposit of one month's rent. Some communities run specials on the deposit for strong applicants. I tell you what move-in special is currently active at each property before you apply, so you are not surprised at signing.

For income qualification, most Ocala communities require gross monthly income of 2.5 to 3 times the monthly rent. On a $1,400 unit, that is $3,500 per month gross, which is well within reach for most registered nurses and allied health professionals. I check the income threshold for each property before putting it on your shortlist.
